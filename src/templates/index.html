Why Secure Passwords Matter?

Keep Hackers Out – Why does complexity matter?

Complex passwords dramatically increase the search space, making brute-force attacks computationally expensive.

Protect Your Data – What am I protecting?

From personal photos to financial info, strong passwords keep everything secure.

Prevent Identity Theft – How do weak passwords expose me?

Reused or guessable passwords let attackers pivot across services and impersonate you.

Stay in Control – Who can access my accounts?

With strong, unique passwords, only you decide who accesses your accounts.

Quick analogy – Is a weak password like a cheap padlock?

Exactly. A weak password is as useless as a flimsy lock. Choose strength. Stay safe.

Best Practices for Strong Passwords:

  • Use at least 12-16 characters (longer is better).
  • Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid common words or personal info (no birthdays, names, or "password123").
  • Consider using a passphrase – four or more random words are easy to remember but hard to crack.
  • Use a password manager to keep track of your credentials securely.
